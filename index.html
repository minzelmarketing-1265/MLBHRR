How the expected HRR & Over probability are calculated

For each batter we estimate three per-plate-appearance rates — a hit rate, a run-scored rate, and an RBI rate — combine them with expected PAs to get an expected H+R+RBI total (μ), then convert μ to the probability of going Over your line using a Poisson approximation.

  1. Per-AB hit rate (b) blends: vs-handedness split (matching the starter's throwing arm), season AVG, and last-5-games AVG, weighted 0.45 / 0.35 / 0.20. Small samples are shrunk toward season/league average (empirical-Bayes). If the batter has 8+ career AB vs the starter, that head-to-head AVG nudges the rate by 15%.
  2. Pitching rate against (pp) blends the opposing starter's BAA (~65%) with the opposing team staff/bullpen BAA (~35%). Thin starter samples are shrunk toward league average.
  3. Matchup combine (log5 / Bill James): p_ab = (b·pp/L) / (b·pp/L + (1−b)(1−pp)/(1−L)). Multiplied by park × weather × fatigue (see below).
  4. Per-PA run rate (rR) = season R/PA, shrunk toward league (~0.118). Scaled by lineup-spot factor (top-of-order scores more): 1→1.13, 2→1.10, 3→1.05, 4→1.00, 5→0.97, 6→0.93, 7→0.90, 8→0.87, 9→0.85. Adjusted by the same opponent/env factors (lighter weight — pitching affects runs less than hits).
  5. Per-PA RBI rate (rI) = season RBI/PA, shrunk toward league (~0.110). Scaled by lineup-spot RBI factor (middle order drives in more): 1→0.83, 2→0.92, 3→1.12, 4→1.18, 5→1.10, 6→1.00, 7→0.93, 8→0.88, 9→0.82.
  6. Expected AB / PA by lineup spot (AB: 1–2 → 4.4, 3 → 4.3, 4–5 → 4.1, 6–7 → 3.9, 8–9 → 3.6; PA ≈ AB × 1.13).
  7. Expected HRR (μ) = AB·p_ab + PA·rR + PA·rI.
  8. Over probability: P(N ≥ ceil(line)) = 1 − Poisson_CDF(ceil(line)−1; μ). Poisson is a reasonable screen for low-count discrete totals; in reality H, R, RBI are positively correlated within a game (good days cluster), so true variance is a bit higher — treat outputs as a screen, not a guarantee.

Environment adjustments (same as the hits model):

  • Park factor — multi-year hits factors per stadium (Coors 1.10, Petco/SF/Sea 0.96, etc.). Applied at full weight to p_ab and at half weight to runs/RBI.
  • Weather — live from Open-Meteo. Average temp 6–9 pm local. weatherMult = 1 + (°F − 70)/1500, clamped to ±3%. Domes (TB, TOR) skip this.
  • Pitcher fatigue — from the starter's recent game log. Short rest worsens opponent BAA: 0–2 days +6%, 3 days +3%, 4–7 neutral, 8–14 +1%, 15+ +2%. Extra +2% if last start ≥ 110 pitches on ≤4 days rest.

The Env column shows the combined park × weather × fatigue multiplier as a single %. Hover for the breakdown.

EV (American odds): implied prob from your line; profit-per-stake from the line; EV = P·profit − (1−P)·stake. Edge = model P − implied P. Expected odds are the fair American odds implied by the model.

This is a statistical model on free public data — not betting advice. Early-season samples are noisy.
