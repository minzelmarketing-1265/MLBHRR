How the expected HRR & Over probability are calculated

For each batter we estimate per-PA hit/run/RBI rates, combine them with expected PAs to get an expected H+R+RBI total (μ), then convert μ to the probability of going Over your line using a Poisson approximation.

  1. Per-AB hit rate (b) blends vs-handedness split, season AVG, and last-5 AVG. Empirical-Bayes shrinkage on small samples. 15% nudge from career H2H vs the starter if 8+ AB.
  2. Pitching rate against (pp) blends starter BAA (~65%) with team staff BAA (~35%).
  3. Log5 / Bill James combine against league BA.
  4. Per-PA run rate (rR) = season R/PA shrunk to ~0.118, scaled by lineup spot (top → more runs).
  5. Per-PA RBI rate (rI) = season RBI/PA shrunk to ~0.110, scaled by lineup spot (3-5 → more RBI).
  6. Environment: park, weather (Open-Meteo), pitcher fatigue. Full weight on hits; sqrt weight on runs/RBI.
  7. Expected HRR (μ) = AB·p_ab + PA·rR + PA·rI, with a global calibration multiplier from backtest.
  8. Over probability: P(N ≥ ceil(line)) = 1 − Poisson_CDF(ceil(line)−1; μ).

See the backtest page for calibration metrics. Current parameters are tuned from a 950-batter-game backtest (Jul 19–28, 2024): global μ-scale 0.93, league priors 0.108 R/PA & 0.104 RBI/PA, and a Platt-style calibration (slope 0.88, +0.02) on Over 0.5/1.5 probabilities to absorb Poisson tail overconfidence. Post-tune: mean predicted μ matches mean actual within 0.01, and all three lines (Over 0.5/1.5/2.5) show positive ROI at p≥55%.
